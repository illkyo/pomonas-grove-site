About Us

Welcome to Pomona's Grove

We are a unique haven dedicated to the artistry of apples. With an unwavering focus on this captivating fruit, we've created a close-knit community that celebrates the allure of nature's bounty. Nestled amid orchards that whisper with stories of ancient orchardists, Pomona's Grove is a gathering place for those who appreciate the subtle complexities of apples. Our members, affectionately known as "Orchard Enthusiasts," share a deep connection to the myriad flavors and experiences that apples offer. From expertly crafted ciders that awaken the palate to guided celebrations of the apple harvest, we invite you to embark on a journey that delves into the rich tapestry of apple heritage. Join us in this vibrant tapestry where we honor the apple's symbolic legacy and cultivate a shared passion for the extraordinary world of apples

Within the embrace of Pomona's Grove, each apple varietal is a masterpiece waiting to be savored. Our orchards are a sanctuary where members can immerse themselves in the art of apple cultivation, learning the delicate balance of nurturing and patience that yields the perfect harvest. From the sweet nostalgia of a Honeycrisp to the enigmatic allure of a Pink Pearl, every apple holds a story, and every bite is an invitation to explore a new chapter. Through hands-on workshops, seasonal festivities, and the camaraderie of fellow Orchard Enthusiasts, Pomona's Grove fosters a shared journey of discovery. It's a place where the love for apples runs deep, and where the spirit of exploration leads to revelations both on the palate and in the heart

The Four Tenants

At Pomona's Grove, our community is guided by four fundamental principles, each as diverse and vibrant as the apples we adore. These tenets, deeply rooted in our orchard heritage, serve as the compass for our collective journey. They encompass the celebration of diversity within our apple-loving family, a commitment to sustainable stewardship of the land, the sharing of knowledge and traditions, and the unwavering belief in the transformative power of apples to connect and inspire. These principles not only define our unique identity but also shape the path toward a more harmonious relationship with nature, the apple, and one another.

Pomona's Grove is not just a place to enjoy apples; it's a living repository of apple wisdom and traditions. We cherish the sharing of knowledge, from age-old apple cultivation techniques passed down through generations to the innovative methods of today. This exchange of wisdom keeps our community thriving and our orchards abundant

The Transformative Power of Apples

We believe in the apple's unique ability to connect and inspire. Beyond their delicious taste, apples hold deep symbolism and cultural significance. They bring people together, spark creativity, and provide nourishment for both body and soul. At Pomona's Grove, we see apples as more than just fruit; they are catalysts for growth, change, and a sense of belonging

Red Delicious

An excellent keeper known for the consistency of its texture, bright red color, and conical shape. It is a sweet apple, lightly aromatic with a firm, cream-colored flesh.

Gala

A red-orange apple with yellow striping, crunchy and juicy, with a sweet, pear-like flavor. Well-suited for snacking, salads, and baking, Gala is ready for picking in September. It tends to darken in color the longer it is stored.

Honeycrisp

Exceptionally juicy with an explosive crunch beyond compare, Honeycrisp has a variable red skin with pale green highlights. Its crispy flesh is cream-colored and a mostly sweet flavor. Honeycrisp is excellent for fresh-eating and in salads. Honeycrisp can be picked as early as late August. It is a good storing apple.

Fuji

A medium to large apple, with orange-red skin. It has some sweetness from its Red Delicious parent, but it is denser and more complex. Its flesh is firm, crisp and juicy. Author Frank Browning includes Fuji in his list of 20 Prize Apples in his book Apples. Fuji is an excellent fresh eating apple, and it is good dried. It is also a great keeper, maintaining its quality for up to one year refrigerated or several weeks left in a fruit bowl. Fuji ripens in late October.

Granny Smith

Is bright green with gray dots, called lenticels, or pores. Its crisp flesh is greenish white, the peel on the tough side. It is a tart apple, good for pies and fresh eating. Although it requires a long growing season, more and more New England orchards are able to grow it. Harvest begins in mid- to late October, into November.

Crispin

Also known as Mutsu, is an excellent dessert, salad, and cider apple. It has a sweet, light flavor when cooked, and it holds its shape well. Can grow quite large. Its skin is yellow with a slight orange blush, and the flesh is white to pale yellow.

Rhode Island Greening

is an excellent pie and all-around cooking apple. It has a green peel and a flesh that is greenish-white with a tart flavor. This heirloom variety is a good substitute for Granny Smith. Usually it is ready for picking in September, but sweetens on the tree if picked in October.

Shizuka

A large, green-yellow apple with a red-orange blush, Shizuka has the same parentage as the Crispin, but its flavor is sweeter, and less tart. Shizuka is firm and juicy, and slow to brown when cut, making it ideal for salads. It ripens in mid-October, and stores well.

Jonagold

is a large, slightly conical apple with an orangish-red blush. Its flesh is explosively crisp and juicy, similar to Honeycrisp but with more apple flavor. Look for Jonagolds in early to mid-October.

Golden Delicious

Though not related to Red Delicious, Golden Delicious shares its shape and many of its leading characteristics. Like the Red, Golden Delicious is a sweet, medium-to-large apple, with pear-like flavor. It is an excellent keeper. Golden Delicious has a greenish-yellow skin that turns gold, and its yellow flesh is crisp and juicy.

Ginger Gold

is sweet, tangy, and juicy. It is round with a smooth green-yellow skin and a slight red blush. Ginger Gold is an excellent multi-purpose eating and cooking apple; it also excels as a salad apple, as the crisp, white flesh browns slowly when sliced. Ginger Gold is harvested as early as late August.

Opal

is a large, sweet, coarsely-grained apple, once widely grown in New England. Harvest begins in mid-September; this multipurpose apple may be enjoyed for only a month or so after picking.

Each apple tells a story of life, growth, and the sweet embrace of simplicity.
